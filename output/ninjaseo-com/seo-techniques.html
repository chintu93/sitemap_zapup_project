Here are some tips on how:

a. Write quality content

  • This ranks high on the list of top SEO techniques. It encompasses great ideas, error-free spelling and grammar and compelling delivery.
  • Use Shorter paragraphs: Generally 3-4 sentence paragraphs are adequate. It could even be one line. But note that an idea or thought should be kept within on paragraph so that it doesn’t get fragmented and lost.
  • Mixed sentence lengths: Though long sentences can be hard to follow especially on a mobile device or computer, they work excellently when used sparingly. Here’s how you should vary your writing:
  • Sub-headers: Informative sub-headers as the average reader will scan through a piece in 37 seconds.
  • Bullet points: If your ideas in a paragraph are more than 3, use bullet points (illustrated here!)
  • White space: As too much white space will overwhelm visitors, leading to increased bounce rates, break it up by adding relevant media such as photographs, graphs and videos.
  • Images/screenshots: Further to breaking monotony, images should be used to illustrate the subject matter, especially if its complex. Here is a YouTube explainer video that gives extra tips on how to create content that ranks.

b) Learn the Art of Bucket Brigades to Enhance the Flow Originally, the term “bucket brigade” was used to refer to people lined up to pass buckets of water when putting out a fire. It has become copywriting technique to keep a readers interest.

Bucket brigade in writing is a cleverly thought out cluster of words used to pique a readers interest and keep the flow. Usually, the bridging flow ends with a colon.

Examples of bucket brigades are:

  • General bucket brigades "Do I have your attention?" "Think about that for a minute…" "I know the feeling:” "OK, I know what you're thinking:"
  • Introductory bucket brigades: "Do you want to learn how to…?" "Have you ever found yourself…?" "Imagine what it would be like:" "Have you wondered why…?"
  • Content body bucket brigades: "Let's get started:" "Let's dig a little deeper:” "Let me explain:" "Let me show you how:"
  • Conclusion bucket brigades: "In short:" "Let's recap:" "In a nutshell:" "But don't take my word for it:"

c) Learn How To Incorporate the Inverted Pyramid Writing Style This method advocates for giving the bulk of important information at the beginning of the article. Readers who only scan articles rarely get to the end, so it makes sense to give them the information straight up. Google knows this so we consider it a top SEO technique as well.
