What are Web Crawlers?

Web crawlers simply refer to employing the use of software to obtain data either from various website pages or a couple of web pages. They are also known as a spider, spider bots, bots, robots, and many more. These names are somewhat descriptive in their functionalities. Which is crawling all over the World Wide Web to the index pages for search engines like Google.

However, search optimization engines do not randomly pinpoint sites subsisting on the net. It involves a process. I will try to explain the process in the best possible way comprehensible even readers that are not tech-savvy. To understand this process you have to know how the search engine works.

The search optimization engine has 3 basic functions namely: ● Crawling: This involves exploring the net for contents and going through each code of every found URL link. Here, the search engines send out a squad of robots to detect up to date contents. These contents could be in the form of a webpage, a video clip, a PDF document, or even an image file that is discovered by URL links. The crawling process is initiated by picking up and following a couple of web pages to find new URL links.

● Indexing: This is the storing and the arrangement of contents discovered in the course of the crawling process. Here, the search engines process and keep information in a database of all discovered contents that seem relevant to the searchers.

●Ranking: This function entails supplying every piece of content that is best relevant to a searcher’s inquiries. When a search is conducted, the search engines dig through for content that is highly relevant to solving searcher’s inquiries. The order in which these contents are arranged by relevance is known as ranking. However, it’s not a secret that the higher a website is ranked in terms of domain authority, the more relevant it is believed by the search engines employed during an inquiry.

Now that you know the basic functions we can now discuss how the process works, The programs are required crawling and indexing before they can be conveyed to the right pages for phrases and keyword search.

Every search engine comes with web crawlers (BOTS) that search through the internet for the indexing of web pages. The BOTS move swiftly from a web page to another looking through all the web pages and making duplicates of each of these pages. The duplicates are kept among other pages that have also been through the crawling process.

Although, there are some instances when the site will go through the crawling process but wouldn’t be indexed. This implies that the crawler was either bugged or delayed. However, they will be indeed in due course.

Why Web Crawlers Is Important

Still wondering what the benefits of web crawlers are? The answer is right here!

By now you should know that your web content can be ranked in a search. You need to be indexed first and the BOTS has to constantly and successfully crawl your website. Any content that has not been indexed will not be found from any subsisting search engines. Just as I mentioned earlier the duplicates of your content have to be in the search engine before it can exist.

Although there are some hassle-free means of getting a website crawled a few times. But with a well-structured website, the crawling process will take place consistently. However, whenever you have new updates on your websites, you have to index your web pages again. This will ensure that it is ranked better. Any modification on your page to a large extent reflects quickly in the search engines. This can be advantageous to your site as the newness and date of the post are one major major ranking factor.

Consistency in the way the search crawl on a website is a significant factor in on-page SEO success. Therefore, you must have a well-structured site that permits the search engines to crawl your website frequently.
