Common affordable crm tools

How to choose the best crm tool with minimal budget

A quality CRM tool is invaluable to business growth. How quality a CRM tool is however depends on how well the tool is able to keep track of customer interactions, maintain strong relationships with new and existing customers, boost sales and improve communication between sales and marketing teams.

Considering the fact that these metrics of measuring quality is widely known to everyone in the CRM marketing space, most vendors have directed their effort towards ensuring this or at least pose to do so. The end result of this is that there’s a really large pool of vendors to pick from.

Choosing the best CRM tool is a tricky task; there are just too many things to think of; ranging from the earlier discussed metrics to other issues like deployment and scalability amongst others. To carry out this task successfully, however, priority should be given to the specific individual needs of the business in question.

Since there’s a lot of things to consider, the following tips should be followed before making any choice:

1. Prioritize user experience:

"Almost all quality improvement comes via simplification of design, manufacturing… layout, processes, and procedures." - Tom Peters

User experience encompasses all features and structures that contribute towards how the user feels when making use of a particular application. User experience and user satisfaction are always in a continuous battle for which metric matters most in determining the quality of CRM marketing.

Before choosing any vendor, it is imperative for you to ask questions like "how enjoyable is the user interface?", "how logical and understandable is the flow of actions?", "how easy is it to use this system?" And many others. These questions and any other ones you can think of are to help you figure out if actually, the CRM tool gives an excellent user experience.

User experience is very important because the whole basis of CRM marketing is to ensure satisfaction. Of what purpose would a crm tool that is a tough nut to crack then be?

2. Carefully outline the features and functionality you need:

although, business growth across different niches can be measured with some common features and growth metrics, nevertheless, each business is unique in its own right and has different characteristics. Since, different customers occupy different business niches, then this is a fact.

Having known that, whatever business or marketing decision made by any business should be tailored specifically to suit that business and consequently scale it. This means, when choosing any CRM marketing vendor, the features and functionalities needed and peculiar to that business must be known. This helps to narrow down the search as only vendors whose features align with the required functionalities will be consulted.

3. Ensure your staffs understand the system or that there is provision for training:

This goes without saying. You can’t get the best results in terms of customer satisfaction, customer experience and sales if your staff does not even understand the CRM tool. Before you commit to a platform, be sure that staffs who will be making use of it actually do understand it or that there is provision for training and retraining.

4. Know how much you are willing to pay:

After looking up the features of a CRM tool, one might get enticed and convinced it is the right one. However, implementing a new CRM system or strategy is a long haul rather than a sprint. Different issues come up along the line thus consuming cash. You need to know how much you can afford to choose and implement that CRM tool that you pick. Assumption can be very detrimental to the growth of the business as seen in the scary Halloween for Hershey.

5. Ensure the crm to be chosen has enough users you can learn from:

This is to help you foresee the hiccups that might be encountered in the course of implementing and using the CRM system. Making use of a new CRM tool might be a tad bit too risky as there is little experience about the platform.

6. Choose crm platforms that fit your organizational scale:

As earlier stated, the most important factor in choosing a CRM tool is a company’s organizational needs and wants. When choosing a CRM tool, you must be sure the tool fits the scale of operation of your organization and that it also fits the future scale of your organization.

7. Set realistic goals:

In business and marketing, success should be viewed more in terms of the journey rather than the destination. After all, CRM is about building and maintaining strong relationships with customers to give them satisfaction and ultimately grow the business. Before you choose any CRM tool, setting realistic goals helps to stay more factual and thus allowing an informed decision or choice of CRM.
