Social Media for Business

Throughout human history, competition and innovation have produced some of the most incredible technologies, solving problems in logistics, healthcare, entertainment, and more. Among those areas most touched by advances in technology, communication has become an integral part of daily life.

Today, social media is an explosive channel that offers benefits at home and at work. The marketing dynamic is every-changing, but every once in a while a development comes along and solidifies a particular technological achievement in time. As social media usage continues to rise to over half of the world’s population, there’s no reason to think it will go away anytime soon.

While communication is at the core of the purpose behind social media, the utility of social platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn, goes far beyond a personal means of communication. Social media entertains us, educates us, connects us, and helps us with everyday tasks.

One such task that has been simplified due to the functions of social media, as well as the prevalence of smartphones, is shopping. Today, consumers have more power in their hands than ever before. According to research, “70% of consumers search for products they need to buy on Instagram and Facebook.” Blazon Additionally, in 2015, 52% of online and in-store purchases were ”influenced by Facebook.”

Customers do more than just shop with social media, however. According to data collected from ReadyCloud, ”71% of online shoppers will recommend the brand they bought on social media.” This mouth-to-mouth means of promotion is invaluable to growing businesses trying to compete in a crowded marketplace. And it works better than you might have imagined. Data from Disruptive Advertising reveals that social media recommendations influence 23% of all online consumers

Social media impacts shopping in a big way. It must be a top-priority in your marketing strategy in order for your brand to have a chance at competing in your industry.

Data from Statista highlights the fact that 91.9 percent of U.S. [businesses with more] than 100 employees were expected to use social media for marketing purposes in 2021.

Social media marketing has become such an important part of business marketing strategy because of the access this channel of marketing provides business owners and marketing and sales teams. More than just mere communication, however, social media also enhances data collection and consumer research.

It may seem obvious that social media platforms enable brands to connect with and engage their users, but the importance of engagement can never be overstated. By delivering engaging content to the right people and at the right time, you can turn readers into leads and leads into paying customers or clients.

In the process of connecting with your audience, if you use social media content best-practices, you can actually entice those readers away from the platform itself, bringing them onto your website. Whether it’s organic posts that bring them to your site or paid ads, the result is the same -- your traffic increases, and that’s always a good thing.

Social media also provides small and medium businesses an opportunity to compete against much larger brands, giving them a level playing field of sorts. On social media, your audience is engaging with the content you deliver. If your content is better than industry leaders, then you’ll have a lasting impact on readers (who are consumers in disguise

However, it’s not as easy as creating a post every once in a while and hoping for the best. Social media strategy can be complex, and it takes knowledge, practice, and flawless execution. It takes data monitoring, data collection, and data analysis. And most importantly, it takes constant optimization to keep your content and your strategies fresh and competitive.

Creating and implementing successful social media strategy is almost impossible without the aid of social media management software like HipSocial.

Purpose of Social Media Management Software

The main purpose of social media management software is to streamline the workflow of marketing and sales teams as it pertains to social media strategy.

There are a number of elements encompassed by social media strategy, and strategies are optimized for individual companies. That means no two companies will have identical social media strategies. It also means that the social media management software market must be diverse enough to serve the wide variety of industries represented throughout the world.

In other words, social media management solutions must come with a variety of features and functions. Additionally, as a rule-of-thumb: versatility and customizability are directly correlated to complexity-of-use. This means that programs that are extremely customizable are going to be more complex and harder to master for new users. Conversely, the less customizable an application is the more user-friendly it’s likely to be.

An exhaustive list of all the features in social media management software would take far too long to cover, but there are several common themes among features that the best social media management solutions always provide:

  • Message Scheduling & Queuing
  • Post Optimization Tools
  • Targeting & Segmentation Tools
  • Seamless Integration
  • URL Shortening & Tracking
  • Reporting & Analytics

These tools, in combination with sound strategy and competent management, lead to a more productive workflow, more communication, and better results.

As we considered the main purpose and functions of social media management software, we turned to problems that our new software should be able to solve and goals it should help us and our users achieve.
