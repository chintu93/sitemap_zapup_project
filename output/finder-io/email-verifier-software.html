How It Works

An email verifier is an online tool or software that allows the user to verify that email addresses are valid and working. If an email address has errors, it can make whatever communications nullified. This can in turn be a waste of time and resources for a business. At worst, it can mean losing a worthwhile opportunity that would have had a significant impact on the business.

The email verifier from finder.io makes this process of verification easy and quick. It enables this process through several ways, some of which we expand upon below.

  • Domain Checking This is a verification that the domain attached to the email address you are trying to reach actually exists and is working. For instance, Gmail accounts are typically considered active as the domain gmail.com is still running. Those that used to have Hotmail email accounts may not, however, be able to access their accounts unless they use Outlook.

  • Syntax Verification This refers to the parameters that make up an email address. If there is an error or omission then the email account trying to be reached likely does not exist. To verify this, the software will examine the spelling, correct positioning of dots and the @ symbol. An email verifier will also look at the domain extension to ensure it is in the correct place.

  • Email Pinging This is the final step in email verification. It involves sending a message from one email server to another. Referred to as an EHLO message, this pinging is designed to check the records of the email domain to ascertain that the email address does exist and is actively in use.
