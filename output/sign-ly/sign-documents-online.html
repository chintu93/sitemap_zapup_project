What is an electronic signature?

An e-signature (electronic signature) is an electronic sound, process or symbol legally attached to a record. It is executed by a person for signing records with the intent to approve the content of an electronic document legally. Such documents are legal, secure, tested, auditable, and verifiable. The process is performed using e-signature software.

Similar to its handwritten counterpart, an e-signature is a legally recognized means of demonstrating the intent of the signer that s/he will adhere to the terms of the contract they’ve signed.

#2 Businesses can customize their signatures for brand promotion.

Good e-signature software supports multiple signature types. There are mainly three different types of electronic signatures used across businesses:

  • Simple electronic signature: This is as simple as writing your name under an e-mail.

  • Advanced electronic signature: Can identify the signatory, and signatory can retain control over the document. All changes within the file are detectable using a public-key infrastructure (PKI) that uses certificates and cryptographic keys.

  • Qualified electronic signature: This is created by a qualified signature creation device. This type of e-signature uses a qualified certificate.
